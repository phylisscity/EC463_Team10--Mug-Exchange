Reusable mugs for online orders

Order online. Pick up in a reusable mug. No BYO cup required.

Today, most reusable cup programs only work if customers order in person and bring their own cup. Mug Exchange lets cafés keep online ordering and pickup, while still handing drinks out in reusable mugs that customers can return later.

The problem we’re addressing

Cafés and coffee shops are under pressure to reduce single-use cup waste. But most existing reusable options ask customers to bring their own cup, order in person, and clean the cup at home.

That means customers lose the convenience of mobile ordering and pickup, and staff have to explain a separate “reusable” process at the counter. As a result, reusable programs stay niche and don’t meaningfully replace single-use cups.

BYO cup only works for a few customers Mobile and web orders stuck with disposables Customers don’t want extra cleanup work

Our solution: Mug Exchange

Mug Exchange makes reusable mugs work with online ordering. Customers place their drink order through the café’s normal digital flow (e.g. food ordering system), choose the Mug Exchange option, and pick up their drink in a café-owned reusable mug—no personal cup required.

  • Order online: Customer checks out as usual, with a “Mug Exchange” option instead of a disposable cup.
  • Pickup: The barista scans the order and scans an RFID-tagged Mug Exchange mug, linking that mug to the order.
  • Return: The customer drops the mug in a smart return bin later. The system marks it as returned and back in circulation.

Why this is different

Instead of asking customers to bring and wash their own cups, Mug Exchange uses café-owned mugs that are tracked and washed by the operator. The customer experience stays simple:

  • No need to carry a cup around all day—just choose Mug Exchange at checkout.
  • Online and in-app orders can finally use reusables, not just in-person orders.
  • Returning is as easy as dropping the mug into a bin on the way out.

How it works (end-to-end)

The Mug Exchange flow is designed to sit alongside your existing digital ordering and café operations.

  • 1. Customer places an order online
    The customer uses your existing ordering channel (web, app, or kiosk). At checkout, they see a “Mug Exchange” option instead of a disposable cup.
  • 2. Order appears at the café
    Your ordering system sends the new order to our backend. The drink is prepared as usual by baristas.
  • 3. Mug is assigned at the counter
    At pickup, the barista scans the order chit and then scans an RFID-tagged Mug Exchange mug. This links that specific mug ID to the customer’s order in our database.
  • 4. Customer leaves with a reusable mug
    The customer takes the drink in the Mug Exchange mug instead of a disposable cup. The mug is now marked as “checked out” in the system.
  • 5. Mug is returned to a smart bin
    When the customer is done, they drop the mug into a return bin with an RFID reader. The system records the return and frees that mug to be washed and reused.
  • 6. Customer can track their activity
    A companion web experience (our prototype SPA) shows the customer their active mug, due time, and history of past Mug Exchange orders.

Technical overview

Under the hood, Mug Exchange is a small full-stack system:

  • Frontend: A mobile-first single-page web app that shows mug status, a history of orders, and participating cafés.
  • Backend: A Node.js server and database that store mug assignments, returns, and customer/link information.
  • Hardware: RFID readers at a counter station and a return bin connect physical mugs to digital orders.

Current status

This is an in-progress prototype built by students as part of a senior design project. We are using campus cafés as an initial test environment to validate the concept before applying it more broadly to cafés and coffee shops.

  • Prototype web app and backend are under development.
  • Hardware integration with RFID readers is in progress.
  • Future goal: integrate with a real food ordering API (e.g. Grubhub) so online orders can be tracked end-to-end with Mug Exchange.

FAQ

Does Mug Exchange replace our existing ordering system?

No. The goal is to sit alongside your current ordering flow. Orders are still placed through your existing web/app experience. Mug Exchange adds a reusable mug option and connects mug assignment and returns to those orders.

Who is responsible for washing the mugs?

The café remains responsible for washing and sanitizing the mugs according to its existing dishwashing processes. Mug Exchange only tracks which mugs are out, which have been returned, and when.

What kind of API integration is required?

In the full version, we expect to receive order identifiers and status updates from your ordering platform via API or webhooks. When a customer selects the Mug Exchange option, that choice is passed to our backend so we can link a mug ID to that order and update it when the mug is returned.

What data do you store about customers?

In the prototype, we only store basic identifiers needed to link orders to mugs (for example, a user ID from the ordering system). The focus is on tracking mug circulation, not detailed customer profiles.

Where are you testing this?

We are currently designing Mug Exchange as a student project and using campus cafés as an initial test environment. The concept is intended for cafés and coffee shops more broadly once validated.

Is this a live production service?

Not yet. This is a prototype and research project. The purpose of this page is to describe the concept, system design, and our plans for integration with real ordering platforms.
